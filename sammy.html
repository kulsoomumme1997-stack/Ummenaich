Case Study 01 · Digital Health Venture · Corporate Venture Build

Sammy — Digital Health Venture

Built and launched a regulated digital health companion from 0→1 inside a 46,000-person FMCG — securing €250K across 3 investment rounds and reaching 45% 4-week retention (1.8× benchmark) in a hard-to-reach 50+ segment.

Opportunity

A stigmatised condition with a clear support gap

Users were managing the condition alone, with limited trusted guidance and fragmented care. Most digital solutions focused on women going through pregnancy or postpartum, leaving older adults, especially men, underserved despite higher stigma and lower digital confidence.

User-voice evidence showed exceptionally high pain acuteness (9/10) and relevance (10/10), confirming the category's desirability and the urgency of solving the problem.

Competitive landscape

The market was thin, not empty: most tools were manual trackers designed for appointment prep, not real-time support. They lacked expert backing, personalisation, and holistic guidance, and for older users — especially men — there was no compelling expert-backed alternative. Most solutions offered only basic exercise reminders and symptom logs, with no personalised programmes or clinical oversight. The real moat was not features, but trust and clinical credibility in a stigmatised, low-confidence segment.

Why now

Demand drivers — aging demographics, stigma-driven self-management, growing regulatory acceptance of digital therapeutics, and emerging reimbursement pathways — created a favourable timing window for a digital companion.

Right-to-win inside a corporate environment

Essity's category expertise, regulatory infrastructure, and brand trust created a right-to-win that startups would struggle to copy. Essity's leadership in a $10–15B global market reinforced its right to own the digital space. The challenge was operating at startup speed without losing the discipline required in a regulated environment.

Venture Thesis

A personalised digital companion for people managing their bladder health and wellness, combining tracking with actionable insights, expert access, and tailored programmes. It provides holistic support across exercise, nutrition, product use, and behavioural guidance, strengthened by emotional support and accountability, to help users improve through small, consistent steps. The personalised programme is grounded in clinically validated methods, aligning the venture with evidence-based care. Backed by Essity's category expertise and trusted product portfolio, the venture creates a guided pathway from understanding to action to product adoption.

Venture Logic

I defined and operationalised three reinforcing loops:

Value Loop: Personalised guidance → visible outcomes → habit → retention
Monetisation Loop: Trust → activation → product upsell (Essity + partners) → premium personalised programmes
Scalability Loop: Data → personalisation → retention → diversified revenue → lower CAC → compounding trust & category advantage

Together, these loops create recurring value in an underserved, high-stigma segment and a commercially scalable model inside a corporate environment.

Adoption feasibility scored 7/10 — strong interest but constrained by digital literacy and accessibility — reinforcing the need for low-friction flows and trust-based onboarding.

GTM Challenge
  • ·Restricted targeting — no direct condition-based acquisition
  • ·No existing playbook — GTM built from zero

Solution-scope analysis confirmed symptom tracking, guided exercises, behavioural education, and product-use support as the highest-value components.

Validation Model

Discovery wasn't a phase — it was the operating system

The riskiest assumption: would a stigmatised 50+ audience trust and engage with a digital companion? Every validation step was designed to answer this before committing capital.

Stage 1 — Problem Exploration

Standard screeners fail when users have spent years not talking about the condition. I redesigned the research approach:

  • ·Trust-building interview framing
  • ·Sequencing questions to reduce shame
  • ·Interviewers matched to user comfort
Outcome: Clear evidence of unmet need around root causes, treatment guidance, and personalised support. But evidence of a problem isn't evidence of willingness to engage with a digital solution — that required a different test.

Operating Cadence

How the venture actually ran

Building Sammy inside a corporate environment required creating the operating system from scratch — combining startup speed with corporate-grade alignment, evidence, and governance.

🔄 Hypothesis-led sprints
Every sprint began with user insight and ended with a validated hypothesis. No build without evidence; no evidence without a test.
🎨 Prototype-first development
All concepts were tested with high-fidelity prototypes (Figma, Lovable, Base44, Claude) before engineering commitment — reducing waste and accelerating learning.
📊 End-to-end analytics from day one
Full visibility across the user journey — funnel, friction, retention, and engagement — driving all product and GTM decisions.
📋 Explicit ownership and documentation
All hypotheses, tests, and outcomes documented and shared across internal teams and external partners. Clear ownership removed friction and accelerated alignment.
⚖️ Regulatory embedded early
Legal and Medical were integrated from the start. GDPR and medical-device constraints shaped the product rather than slowing it — enabling speed and compliance.
🏛️ Quarterly investment governance
C-suite reviews every three months tied progress to retention, unit economics, and market potential. Secured three consecutive funding approvals.
⚡ A compounding test–learn–iterate loop
No decision without a hypothesis. No hypothesis without a test. Each cycle built compounding insight across product, GTM, and research — creating a repeatable operating system for venture scale.
Proof Points

Metrics that justified continued investment

The goal was simple: build something users valued enough to scale — and prove it with data before each committee review. Every metric below contributed directly to securing continued investment.

€250K across three C-suite rounds
Evidence-based buy-in from an FMCG C-suite for a digital health venture. I owned the full €250K budget, with each approval tied to traction, unit economics, and KPI progress.
The paywall failed — and that was the insight
The MVP launched with a freemium subscription and paywall. The data was blunt: a 50+ audience with low digital confidence, managing a stigmatised condition, wouldn't commit to payment — many wouldn't even take the free tier. The blocker was trust and security: users hesitated to share payment and personal data before confidence was earned. I killed the paywall, reframed monetisation as downstream of activation, and rebuilt the funnel around engagement and retention. Willingness-to-pay had already been validated in the smoke test; launch data proved that trust precedes revenue for this segment.
45% 4-week retention — 1.8× category benchmark
In a category where most users drop off in week one. Retention improved through continuous personalisation, expert-backed content, smart nudges, and a progress system that made outcomes visible.
Activation uplift: 40% → 60%
A 50% relative increase for a 50+ audience with low digital confidence. Onboarding was rebuilt for instant value and the shortest possible time-to-value: targeted, low-friction flows designed for low digital confidence, delivering personalised guidance within health-compliance constraints.
CAC €12 → €6 · CTR 13% (6× benchmark)
Across 12+ experiments on restricted channels — no agency, no scale budget. Optimised creatives, messaging, audiences, and channels through iteration alone. CTR reached 13% against a 2% industry benchmark in a category where most platforms restrict condition-based targeting entirely.
V1 → V2 pivot validated
Research showed the structured programme remained valuable, but Wizard-of-Oz testing validated expert chat as a lower-friction, more human layer that supported habitual daily use. V2 improved differentiation, strengthened retention, and shaped the broader business-unit strategy.
Where it landed
The validated venture transitioned into the business unit with a recommendation to scale as a holistic digital companion — combining core features with integrated expert chat — carrying the V1 → V2 evidence directly into the unit's roadmap.
